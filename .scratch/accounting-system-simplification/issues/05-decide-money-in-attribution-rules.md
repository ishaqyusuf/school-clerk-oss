## Question

How should money-in be attributed when the collection term and the paid-for term differ?

Resolve:
- how a payment collected in the current active term should record that it is paying for a previous term;
- whether the cash balance belongs to the currently open term ledger once the previous term is closed;
- whether the prior term balance should be reduced, annotated, or left closed with a later recovery note;
- how receipts should display collected date, collected term, paid-for term, and affected account;
- how student arrears from previous terms should appear in the current term bursary workspace;
- whether closed terms can receive direct ledger entries or only adjustment/carry-forward entries in the active term.

The answer should define the rule for late student payments and any other late income without confusing cash reporting.

## Approved direction

Every student payment needs two meanings:

- cash was **collected in** this term;
- debt/payment was **for** this term.

If a student pays last term's fee during the new term, the cash belongs to the current open term ledger, but the payment allocation references the old term charge.

Receipts should show:

- payment date;
- collected in term;
- paid for term;
- account affected;
- amount paid.

Closed previous terms should not receive new cash ledger entries directly.
